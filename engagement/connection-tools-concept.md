# Connection Tools for Instructors — Concept

**Status:** Concept · target readiness **January 2027** (build alongside Phase 2 engagement)
**Principle:** Connection, not solicitation. We are NOT asking instructors to fundraise. We're giving them simple, dignified ways to share their passion — connected to NRM — with clients and parents, and letting the client's own curiosity do the rest.

---

## The idea in one line

Equip every instructor to turn a meaningful lesson into a moment of *connection* — awareness of and affinity for NRM — without ever asking anyone for money.

The instructor's entire job is one sentence:
> *"This lesson meant a lot to me too — would you mind if I shared this story with my professional organization?"*

From there, the client pulls: they ask more, they visit the site, they follow along. **Awareness and affinity now; the campaign can build on it in 2027.** The instructor never has to "sell" anything.

---

## The signature moment (the flow we're designing for)

1. **A real lesson lands** — a kid links turns, a nervous parent watches their child succeed, an adult conquers a fear.
2. **The instructor names it** — "that mattered to me too." Human, true, easy.
3. **The soft share** — "would you mind if I shared this with my org?" (consent-first; the client feels honored, not pitched.)
4. **The client pulls** — they're curious. The instructor points them somewhere simple (a card, a QR, a profile link).
5. **The site does the work** — the client lands on something warm: the instructor's profile, the "Why I Teach" stories, what NRM is. They follow / sign up / share.
6. **The loop closes** — that moment may itself become a story (with consent), feeding the next instructor's share.

No money is mentioned anywhere in this flow. That's the point.

---

## Starter toolkit (simple, low-friction)

| Tool | What it is | Why it works |
|------|-----------|--------------|
| **Instructor profile page** | Every instructor already has a profile on the new site — make it the thing they point clients to | Already built; it's *their* page, so sharing feels personal, not promotional |
| **"Follow my work" card / QR** | A small physical or digital card an instructor hands a client: name + QR to their profile or a client-facing landing page | Zero friction in the moment; client scans and explores on their own time |
| **Client-facing landing page** | A warm "you just had a lesson with an NRM pro — here's who we are" page (stories, mission, follow/subscribe), *not* a donate page | Meets the curious client where they are; awareness, not ask |
| **One-line consent script** | The exact phrasing instructors can use ("would you mind if I shared this story?") on a wallet card or in onboarding | Removes the awkwardness; makes the soft-share repeatable |
| **"Share a moment" capture** | A dead-simple form/link where an instructor (with consent) submits a story or photo from a lesson | Sources member-generated content + Why-I-Teach stories; the connection moment becomes an asset |
| **Follow / newsletter / Discord opt-in** | Easy ways for a client or parent to stay connected to NRM | Converts a single moment into an ongoing relationship |

## What the site needs to support this

Much of it already exists in the build — this is mostly assembly, not new infrastructure:

- ✅ **Member profiles** — done (the share destination)
- ✅ **"Why I Teach" stories gallery** — planned (the emotional proof for curious clients)
- ⬜ **Client-facing landing page** — new, lightweight; "you skied with an NRM pro"
- ⬜ **Per-instructor share link / QR generator** — small feature; could be as simple as a profile URL + a printable card template
- ⬜ **"Share a moment" submission + light moderation** — overlaps with the member-generated content step already in the plan
- ⬜ **Follow/subscribe path** — newsletter (MailChimp) + Discord + socials

- **Phase 2 (now–fall 2026):** build the site pieces, seed the stories, pilot the soft-share with a few willing instructors (Katie/Brenna/Jill cohort are natural early adopters).
- **By Jan 2027:** the toolkit is live and a handful of instructors are already using it — so when Our Mountain launches, *Connection is a habit, not a request.* The campaign borrows from a warm, aware audience instead of starting cold.
- **Ties to the Our Mountain "Pocket Talking Points":** that doc already tells board/instructors "you're not asking for money — you're making connections." This is the toolkit that makes that real, a year early.
