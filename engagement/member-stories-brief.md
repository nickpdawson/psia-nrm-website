# "Why I Teach" — Member Story Series Brief

**Status:** Concept locked · sourcing next
**Owner:** Nick (produces) · **Format:** 7–8 short videos
**Strategic role:** This is the **Connection (C1)** asset for the January 2027 Our Mountain launch. Build it now during Phase 2; deploy it again during the campaign.

---

## The idea

Short, story-driven videos of NRM members answering one simple question: *why do you teach?* Each one centers on a real, specific moment — a kid who finally linked turns, a client who came back from injury, a lesson that mattered more than the skiing. Not technique, not credentials — the human reason people do this.

**Why it matters twice:**
1. **Now (engagement):** authentic, emotional content that makes members feel seen and gives non-members a reason to care. Highly shareable.
2. **Jan 2027 (philanthropy):** the campaign's Three Cs ask board/instructors to surface *Connection*. This library *is* Connection, pre-made — ready to anchor the launch instead of starting cold.

---

## Casting — aim for range (Jill's point)

Pick 7–8 members who, together, cover the breadth of NRM. Deliberately mix:

- **Career stage:** a younger/newer examiner *and* a longtime/older member
- **Discipline:** alpine, snowboard, adaptive, telemark, nordic
- **Geography:** big resorts (Big Sky, Bridger) *and* small/isolated hills (Blacktail, Snowbowl) — the isolation theme from the member research
- **Role:** an ed-staff/examiner, a rank-and-file instructor, maybe a ski-school director
- **Story type:** at least one adaptive/Eagle-Mount story, one "came back to it later in life" story, one "a kid I taught" story

> The range is the point: the more kinds of members who see themselves in the series, the wider the Connection net for January.

## Format

- **Length:** 60–90 seconds each (vertical 9:16 for socials; can also cut a 2–3 min "long" version for the site)
- **Look:** simple, warm, real — on-mountain or at their home hill if possible; natural light; lav mic
- **Structure (each video):**
  1. Name, hill, what they teach (a beat)
  2. The moment — *tell me about a lesson or a person that stuck with you*
  3. Why that's why they teach
  4. Close on the member, not the logo
- **Series wrapper:** consistent title card ("Why I Teach · PSIA-NRM"), same font/lower-third, so 8 clips read as one series

---

## Interview prompts (pick 2–3 per person)

- Tell me about a lesson you'll never forget.
- Was there a student — a kid or an adult — who changed how you think about teaching?
- What's a moment that reminded you why you do this?
- Who taught *you*, and what did they give you?
- When someone "gets it" in front of you — what's that like?
- What would the mountains lose if instructors like you couldn't afford to stay? *(bridges quietly toward Our Mountain — use only where it feels natural)*

Keep it conversational. The best lines come after the "official" answer — keep the camera rolling.

---

## Production workflow (low-lift, batchable)

1. Build the cast list (Nick + Jill help recruit; target 8, expect a couple to fall through).
2. Cluster shoots by hill — knock out 2–3 interviews per location visit.
3. Shoot loose: 10–15 min of conversation per person → cut to the one best story.
4. Edit as a consistent series; release on a cadence (e.g., one every 1–2 weeks) to keep the site/socials fresh through fall.
5. **Tag and archive** every raw + cut file so they're ready to re-deploy for the Jan 2027 campaign.
