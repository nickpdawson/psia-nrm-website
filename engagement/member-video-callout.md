# Member Video Call-Out — "Send Us Your Skiing" (Brenna MA series)

**Purpose:** Solicit member ski videos → Nick curates 4–6 → Brenna records voice-only movement analysis → published on the new site + socials. Kicks off member-generated content and feeds Brenna's series.
**Assets on hand:** Brenna headshot + a clip of Brenna skiing (use in the email header / IG post).

---

## Submissions plan — Dropbox File Request (recommended)

**Use a Dropbox File Request.** Best tool for this job:
- One link; submitters need **no account**.
- They **can't see** each other's files (private).
- Handles large video files.
- Everything lands in one folder for you.

**Setup:** Dropbox → *File requests* → *Create request* → title "NRM Movement Analysis" → choose/create a destination folder → copy the link. Ask submitters to name their file `First-Last_HomeHill`.

**Why not Google:** a Google Form file-upload question forces submitters to sign into a Google account (friction); a plain Drive folder doesn't allow clean anonymous uploads. If you must stay in Google, a Form works — just expect the sign-in drop-off.

**Consent:** submitting = they're OK with us using it. Include the one-line note below asking them to only send footage they have rights to, and to have permission if a student/client (especially a minor) is identifiable.

3. She records a voice-over breakdown — what she sees, what she'd cue, how to level up.
4. We share the sessions on the new NRM site and our socials so everyone learns.

It's free, it's a rare chance to get eyes from a National Team coach, and you might just see yourself featured.

**Send your video here:** [DROPBOX FILE REQUEST LINK]

A few filming tips:
- 30–90 seconds, a run or two on terrain that pushes you a little.
- Film from the side or a follow-cam if you can, and keep yourself in frame head-to-ski.
- Good light and visibility (skip the flat-light whiteout days).
- Name your file `First-Last_YourHill`.

Quick note: only send footage you have the rights to, and if a student or anyone else is clearly identifiable — especially a kid — please make sure you have their okay first.

Submit by [deadline].

Can't wait to see your turns.

Nick
PSIA-NRM
